# Look

## 1. Purpose
This document defines the visual system of the site.

## 2. Decisions Owned
- The overall visual direction
- The color system
- The typography system
- Page-specific layout rhythm
- Link, image, caption, and audio presentation
- Mobile behavior

## 3. Requirements

### 3.1 Visual Direction
1. The site feels closer to a printed book or small press publication than to a blog or app.
2. Type, spacing, and hierarchy carry the design. Ornament is minimal.

### 3.2 Color And Material
1. The page background uses a warm paper tone and the text uses a dark ink tone with strong reading contrast.
2. The site uses one muted accent color for links, rules, and small emphasis.
3. Thin rules may separate sections, but heavy borders, panels, shadows, textures, badges, and glossy effects are avoided.

### 3.3 Typography
1. The primary reading typeface is a serif.
2. The site may use one quiet secondary face for the global links block, labels, and dates.
3. The home title has the strongest scale on the site.
4. Body copy uses a comfortable reading measure on large screens.
5. Meta text is quieter than body copy and titles.
6. Links stay clearly distinct from surrounding copy.

### 3.4 Page Rhythm
1. The homepage uses a title-page rhythm with generous top spacing and a compact content stack centered on the title-page content.
2. The works index uses a table-of-contents rhythm with visible section breaks and tight, scannable entries.
3. Work pages use a single reading column.
4. The global links block may use a more expanded treatment on the homepage and a more compact treatment on other pages.

### 3.5 Media Treatment
1. Images sit cleanly in the page flow with simple, unframed treatment.
2. Captions are smaller than body text and visually tied to their media.
3. Audio players align with the reading column and have enough surrounding space to feel intentional.

### 3.6 Mobile Behavior
1. The visual system works on mobile and desktop without separate templates.
2. On small screens, spacing tightens before type becomes cramped.
3. The layout avoids horizontal scrolling for the global links block and reading content. Images and audio controls scale to the available width.

## 4. Acceptance Checks
1. Confirm that the visual system reads as warm, editorial, and restrained rather than product-like.
2. Confirm that the homepage feels like a title page and the works index feels like a table of contents.
3. Confirm that the design uses a paper-toned background, dark text, and one muted accent color.
4. Confirm that serif-led typography carries the design.
5. Confirm that work pages keep a clear reading column.
6. Confirm that the design works cleanly on small screens without horizontal scrolling.
7. Confirm that images, captions, and native audio controls follow the same calm visual language.

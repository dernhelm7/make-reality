Essay · November 2024

The Folded Map

There is a way of reading a place that has nothing to do with being there.1 You fold the map until only one square shows, and you study it until you know it better than the territory it describes.

The map is always a more forgiving version of the land. It does not have weather. It does not have the sun in your eyes or the sound of the river you cannot see from where you are standing.2

To read a map is to read a theory of a place. And like all theories, it is most useful precisely where it is most wrong.

1 Rebecca Solnit makes a version of this argument in A Field Guide to Getting Lost.
2 The Ordnance Survey maps of Ireland famously omit the sound of the sea.
